# Veritasium

Slash: `/veritasium`

Before starting, read [shared-rules.md](../../video-ideas/shared-rules.md).

## Ethos

Curiosity-gap science storytelling: open with a weird observable fact or counterintuitive question, then earn the explanation. Audience contract = "I didn't know that was possible / that wrong / that hard." Titles promise a mystery, a paradox, or an impossible-seeming thing made tangible.

## Top titles

- Why Are 96,000,000 Black Balls on This Reservoir?
- The Simplest Math Problem No One Can Solve - Collatz Conjecture
- World's Lightest Solid!
- Why It Was Almost Impossible to Make the Blue LED
- Something Strange Happens When You Follow Einstein's Math
- Can You Keep Zooming In Infinitely?
- Can You Swim in Shade Balls?
- I Waterproofed Myself With Aerogel!
- The Man Who Accidentally Killed The Most People In History
- How An Infinite Hotel Ran Out Of Room
- Inside The Navy's Indoor Ocean
- How Microwaving Grapes Makes Plasma

## Steps

1. Take user's expertise / experience / topic.
2. Read Top titles. For each (or a strong subset), transpose the *structure* onto the user's world.
3. Ask implicitly: "What would Veritasium do if they focused on [user's thing]?"
4. Output a list of suggested titles (and optional one-line topic notes). Stop. User picks.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't copy their niche content; transpose the title grammar
- Don't expand into scripts or production plans
- Don't skip the Top titles list and improvise "vibes only"
- Don't claim endorsement or impersonate
