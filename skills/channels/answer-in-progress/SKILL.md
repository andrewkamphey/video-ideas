# Answer in Progress

Slash: `/answer-in-progress`

Before starting, read [shared-rules.md](../../video-ideas/shared-rules.md).

## Ethos

Whimsical why-essays plus prove-a-point process videos. Titles sound like lowercase curiosity ("why X is Y") or a personal experiment to test a belief. Audience contract = "we'll follow a slightly absurd question to a real answer."

## Top titles

- why america is addicted to cars
- i did art fraud to prove a point
- why you were forced to learn the recorder in school
- why millennials have no taste
- I taught an AI to solve the trolley problem

## Steps

1. Take user's expertise / experience / topic.
2. Read Top titles. For each (or a strong subset), transpose the *structure* onto the user's world.
3. Ask implicitly: "What would Answer in Progress do if they focused on [user's thing]?"
4. Output a list of suggested titles (and optional one-line topic notes). Stop. User picks.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't copy their niche content; transpose the title grammar
- Don't expand into scripts or production plans
- Don't skip the Top titles list and improvise "vibes only"
- Don't claim endorsement or impersonate
