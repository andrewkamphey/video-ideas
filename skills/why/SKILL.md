# Why

Slash: `/why`

Before starting, read [shared-rules.md](../video-ideas/shared-rules.md).

## Purpose

Dig five layers of why to sharpen a video idea. Start with a concept and keep asking why until the core is exposed.

## Inputs

One concept, topic, or rough video idea.

## Mode

`interactive`. One why per turn; wait for the user's answer before the next layer.

## Steps

1. Ask for the concept / topic / rough idea.
2. Ask: Why? (or: Why should this exist? / Why is that the case?)
3. Take the user's answer. Ask why of *that* answer.
4. Repeat until depth 5 (or user signals "that's the core").
5. Reflect back the sharpened idea or title spark from the deepest layer.

## Stop

Five whys reached, or user names the core. Offer one sharpened idea/title. User picks whether to develop.

## Output

Sharpened idea or title spark from the why chain. Not a full script.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not answer the whys for the user. They own the expertise
- Do not skip layers or batch all five whys in one turn
- Do not expand into a full outline or content calendar
