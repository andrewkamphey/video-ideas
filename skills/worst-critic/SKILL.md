# Worst Critic

Slash: `/worst-critic`

Before starting, read [shared-rules.md](../video-ideas/shared-rules.md).

## Purpose

Surface the harshest criticism. Make videos from facing it, not dodging it.

**Stutz framing (use verbatim):**  
"Part X is your worst critic. It's the part of you that declares how impossible your goals are, blocks you from making progress and has a complete dossier of who you are and who you are not."

## Inputs

Optional domain/context. The user's own critic voice, not invented attacks.

## Mode

`interactive`. Wait between steps.

## Steps

1. Ask: What's the worst thing someone would say to you / criticize you for? (Optionally name Part X / Worst Critic.)
2. Let the user answer. Push once for the dossier: specific, cruel, precise, not vague "I'm not good enough."
3. Hold the stark frame: this worst critic might be right.
4. In the face of that, ask in sequence (wait between each):
   - What do we do?
   - What do we want to do?
   - What videos do we want to make?
   - What do we want to make to prove?
5. Turn answers into concrete video ideas / titles. Proof-oriented, not motivational fluff.

## Stop

User has a short list of "prove it" video ideas, or declines to go deeper.

## Output

Concrete proof-oriented video ideas / titles. User picks.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not therapize or reassure away the critic
- Do not skip to nice titles before the critic is named clearly
- Do not produce generic "believe in yourself" content. Bias toward evidence, demos, receipts, process receipts
